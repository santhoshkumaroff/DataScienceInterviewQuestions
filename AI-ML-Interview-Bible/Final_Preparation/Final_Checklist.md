# Final Interview Checklist

## Your Complete Day-by-Day Guide to Interview Success

This checklist covers everything from the week before your interview to the follow-up after. Print this out and tick items as you complete them.

---

# Part 1: One Week Before the Interview

## Company Research

- [ ] Read the company website (About, Products, Blog, Careers page)
- [ ] Read the job description again - highlight key requirements
- [ ] Research company culture (Glassdoor, LinkedIn, YouTube)
- [ ] Read recent company news (Google News search)
- [ ] Find your interviewers on LinkedIn (know their background)
- [ ] Check company tech stack (builtwith.com, StackShare)
- [ ] Try the company's product if available (use it for 30 mins)
- [ ] Note 2-3 questions to ask about the company

## Resume & Profile Prep

- [ ] Re-read your own resume thoroughly
- [ ] Be ready to explain every bullet point
- [ ] Update LinkedIn profile to match resume
- [ ] Ensure GitHub portfolio is clean and updated
- [ ] Add README files to all important projects
- [ ] Remove any embarrassing or incomplete repos from public view

## Technical Revision Plan

- [ ] Review ML fundamentals (supervised/unsupervised, bias-variance, evaluation metrics)
- [ ] Review your own projects (architecture, algorithms used, results)
- [ ] Practice coding in Python (arrays, strings, hash maps, recursion)
- [ ] Implement 2-3 ML algorithms from scratch (Linear Regression, KNN, Logistic Regression)
- [ ] Practice SQL (basic queries, joins, aggregations)
- [ ] Review statistics basics (mean, median, standard deviation, probability, distributions)
- [ ] Review deep learning concepts if relevant (CNN, RNN, LSTM, Transformer basics)

## STAR Story Preparation

- [ ] Prepare 3-5 stories using STAR method:
  - [ ] Story 1: Technical challenge you overcame
  - [ ] Story 2: Team conflict or collaboration
  - [ ] Story 3: Failure and what you learned
  - [ ] Story 4: Success / achievement
  - [ ] Story 5: Time you showed leadership

## Logistics Preparation

- [ ] Confirm interview date, time, and timezone
- [ ] Confirm interview format (phone, video, in-person)
- [ ] For video: test camera, microphone, internet connection
- [ ] For in-person: plan route, check travel time, parking
- [ ] Note down interviewer names and roles
- [ ] Get contact info for the recruiter (phone/email)

---

# Part 2: One Day Before the Interview

## Rest & Preparation

- [ ] Go to bed early (aim for 7-8 hours of sleep)
- [ ] Prepare your outfit (see "What to Wear" section below)
- [ ] Pack your bag with all necessities
- [ ] Charge your laptop, phone, and any devices
- [ ] Don't study new topics (review what you already know)
- [ ] Do something relaxing (walk, music, light exercise)
- [ ] Eat a healthy dinner (avoid heavy or unfamiliar food)
- [ ] Prepare water bottle for the interview

## What to Wear

| Interview Type | Recommendation |
|---------------|---------------|
| In-person at corporate | Formal: Shirt + trousers + closed shoes (or saree/salwar for women) |
| In-person at startup | Smart casual: Solid color shirt, neat jeans/trousers |
| Video interview | Formal from waist up (shirt/blazer), but comfortable below |
| Phone interview | Dress comfortably (it helps mentally) |

## Materials to Pack

- [ ] Printed resume (3-4 copies)
- [ ] Notepad and pen (for notes during interview)
- [ ] Government ID (Aadhar, Passport, Driver's License)
- [ ] Portfolio/Project list (printed)
- [ ] Water bottle
- [ ] Laptop and charger (if asked to bring)
- [ ] Phone charger / power bank

## Documents Checklist

- [ ] Resume (3-4 copies)
- [ ] Photo ID proof
- [ ] Degree certificates / provisional certificates
- [ ] Internship offer letters / experience letters
- [ ] Previous employer documents (if applicable)
- [ ] Portfolio / GitHub links ready

## Final Review

- [ ] Read job description one more time
- [ ] Review your 3-5 STAR stories out loud
- [ ] Practice "Tell me about yourself" (60 seconds, record yourself)
- [ ] Review 2-3 questions YOU want to ask
- [ ] Set 2 alarms (one primary, one backup)
- [ ] Inform family/housemates about your interview time
- [ ] Check weather forecast for your commute

---

# Part 3: Day of the Interview

## Morning Routine

- [ ] Wake up early (at least 3 hours before interview)
- [ ] Have a light, healthy breakfast
- [ ] Take a shower (feeling fresh = feeling confident)
- [ ] Wear your prepared outfit
- [ ] Do a light review (don't cram, just glance at notes)
- [ ] Do breathing exercises (5 deep breaths)
- [ ] Leave early if in-person (arrive 15-20 mins before)

## For Video Interviews (Extra Steps)

- [ ] Find a quiet room with NO distractions
- [ ] Test internet connection (speedtest.net)
- [ ] Check camera and microphone (Zoom/Google Meet test)
- [ ] Clean background (plain wall or virtual background)
- [ ] Good lighting (face the window or use a ring light)
- [ ] Close all other browser tabs and applications
- [ ] Put phone on silent / Do Not Disturb mode
- [ ] Have water and tissues nearby
- [ ] Keep resume visible (printed, next to screen)

## 30 Minutes Before

- [ ] Go to the bathroom (seriously, do this)
- [ ] Open the interview link/software
- [ ] Have the recruiter's contact number ready
- [ ] Get yourself calm and centered
- [ ] Smile (it changes your energy and voice)

# Part 4: During the Interview

## Body Language Tips

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Sit up straight | Slouch or lean back |
| Maintain eye contact (look at camera for video) | Look down or away constantly |
| Smile naturally | Frown or look too serious |
| Use hand gestures naturally | Cross arms or fidget |
| Nod to show listening | Interrupt the interviewer |
| Speak clearly and at moderate pace | Speak too fast or too softly |

## How to Handle Questions

**When you know the answer:**
1. Pause briefly (2-3 seconds)
2. Structure your answer (first this, then that)
3. Be concise but complete
4. Ask "Does that answer your question?" at the end

**When you don't know the answer:**
1. Don't panic (it's okay!)
2. Say: "That's an interesting question. Let me think about it."
3. Share what you DO know related to the topic
4. Be honest: "I haven't worked with that specific technique, but based on my understanding..."

**What to NEVER say:**
- "I don't know" and stop talking
- Make up something you're unsure about
- "That wasn't covered in my course"
- Say negative things about past experiences

## The STAR Method (REMINDER)

| Letter | Meaning | Example Starter |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| S | Situation | "In my final year project..." |
| T | Task | "We needed to detect crop diseases with limited data..." |
| A | Action | "I researched data augmentation techniques and implemented..." |
| R | Result | "We achieved 94% accuracy, a 22% improvement..." |

## Note-Taking During Interview

- [ ] Keep a notepad handy
- [ ] Write down key points the interviewer mentions
- [ ] Note down any technical terms you want to look up later
- [ ] Write down questions you want to ask at the end
- [ ] Jot down feedback or comments for self-reflection

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer

**Pick 2-3 from below:**

- "What does success look like for this role in the first 6 months?"
- "What's the team culture like? How does the team collaborate?"
- "What are the biggest technical challenges the team is working on?"
- "How does the company support learning and development?"
- "What does a typical day look like for someone in this role?"
- "How do you approach model deployment and monitoring here?"
- "What technology stack does the team use?"
- "What's the next step in the interview process?"

## Topics to AVOID Asking About

- Salary, benefits, vacation time (wait for HR round)
- "How did I do in this interview?"
- Questions that show you didn't research the company
- Negative questions about company culture or management
- "When will I get promoted?"

# Part 5: After the Interview

## Immediately After

- [ ] Thank the interviewer(s) for their time
- [ ] Ask about next steps and timeline
- [ ] Write down everything you remember (questions, answers, feedback)
- [ ] Note questions you struggled with (study these)
- [ ] Reflect on what went well and what could improve
- [ ] Take a deep breath and reward yourself!

## Follow-Up Email (Send within 24 hours)

**Template:**

Subject: Thank You - [Role Name] Interview

Dear [Interviewer Name],

Thank you for taking the time to meet with me today. I really enjoyed learning more about the [Role Name] position and the work your team is doing at [Company Name].

Our conversation reinforced my excitement about this opportunity. I was particularly interested in [specific thing you discussed], and I'm confident my skills in [your skill] can contribute meaningfully to your team.

Please let me know if there are any additional steps in the process. I look forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,

[Your Name]
[Phone Number]
[LinkedIn Profile Link]

---

## Self-Reflection Journal

After the interview, write down:

1. **What went well?**
   - Which questions did you answer well?
   - When did you feel confident?

2. **What could be better?**
   - Which questions were difficult?
   - Where did you feel stuck or nervous?

3. **What did you learn?**
   - New technical concepts mentioned
   - Areas you need to study more
   - Interview techniques to improve

4. **Next steps:**
   - Topics to review
   - Skills to practice
   - Next interview to prepare for

# Bonus: Common Interview Day Mistakes to Avoid

## Mistake 1: Arriving Late
**Fix:** Plan to arrive 20 minutes early. Account for traffic, weather, and getting lost.

## Mistake 2: Forgetting Documents
**Fix:** Pack everything the night before. Use a checklist.

## Mistake 3: Bad Internet (Video)
**Fix:** Test connection 1 hour before. Have mobile hotspot as backup.

## Mistake 4: Not Testing Audio/Video
**Fix:** Join the meeting link 5 minutes early to test.

## Mistake 5: Rambling Answers
**Fix:** Pause, think, then answer. Keep answers to 60-90 seconds.

## Mistake 6: Interrupting
**Fix:** Wait 2 seconds after interviewer finishes speaking before you start.

## Mistake 7: Negative Body Language
**Fix:** Sit up straight, smile, maintain eye contact.

## Mistake 8: Not Having Questions
**Fix:** Always prepare 3-4 questions beforehand.

## Mistake 9: Forgetting to Follow Up
**Fix:** Send thank you email within 24 hours.

## Mistake 10: Overthinking After the Interview
**Fix:** Write down your reflections, then let it go. Focus on the next opportunity.
