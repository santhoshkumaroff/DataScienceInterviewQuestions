# Salary Negotiation Guide for Freshers

## Your Complete Guide to Getting What You Deserve

Talking about money can be scary, especially as a fresher. But here's the truth: **salary negotiation is normal, expected, and you won't lose an offer by negotiating professionally.** Let's learn how.

---

# Part 1: When to Discuss Salary

## The Right Timing

| Stage | What to Say | What NOT to Do |
|-------|------------|----------------|
| Application Form | Write "Negotiable" or a broad range | Don't give a fixed number |
| Phone Screen | Deflect politely (see script below) | Don't demand a specific number |
| Technical Rounds | Don't discuss salary at all | Don't bring it up yourself |
| HR Round | Discuss only if asked | This is the right time |
| Offer Stage | **This is when you negotiate** | This is your best opportunity |
| After Offer | Negotiate within 3-5 days | Don't wait too long |

## Script: How to Deflect Salary Questions Early

**Interviewer:** "What are your salary expectations?"

**You (during phone screen):**
"I'm flexible based on the role and overall compensation. I'd prefer to understand the responsibilities better before discussing numbers. What's the budgeted range for this position?"

**You (during technical round):**
"I'd like to focus on understanding the role and showing my skills first. I'm sure we can discuss compensation when the time is right."

# Part 2: How to Research Market Rates

## Sources for Salary Research

| Source | What to Look For | How to Use |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| Glassdoor | Company-specific salaries | Search "[Company] AI/ML Engineer salary" |
| AmbitionBox | Indian market salaries | Search by company and role |
| LinkedIn Salary | Range by location and experience | Use LinkedIn's Salary tool |
| Naukri / Indeed | Average salary ranges | Compare multiple listings |
| Levels.fyi | FAANG and top tech salaries | Check total compensation |
| Friends / Seniors | Real, insider data | Ask politely and anonymously |
| Reddit (r/developersIndia) | Community salary discussions | Search for your city + role |

## How to Calculate Your Range

**Formula for freshers:**
1. Find the minimum market rate for your role + city
2. Find the maximum market rate
3. Your range: (min + 10%) to (max - 5%)

**Example for ML Engineer fresher in Bangalore:**
- Market range: 6 LPA to 12 LPA
- Your range: 6.5 LPA to 11 LPA
- Your target: 8-9 LPA

# Part 3: How to Answer "What's Your Expected Salary?"

## Strategy: Give a Range, Not a Number

**Bad answer:** "I expect 8 LPA."
**Good answer:** "Based on my research and industry standards, I'm looking for a package in the range of 7 to 10 LPA."

## Why a Range Works
- It shows you've done research
- It gives room for negotiation
- It doesn't lock you into one number
- The lower end sets your minimum
- The upper end gives you room to be happy with an offer in the middle

## What to Say in Different Situations

**If you have multiple offers:**
"I have another offer at X LPA. However, your company is my first choice, and I'd love to join if we can find a mutually agreeable number."

**If this is your first job:**
"As a fresher, my priority is learning and growth. Based on market research, I'm looking at a range of 6 to 9 LPA. I'm also interested in the overall learning opportunities here."

**If you're from a tier-3 college / non-traditional background:**
"I understand the market standard for this role is X-Y. I'm confident in my skills based on my projects and would like to be compensated fairly for the value I bring."

# Part 4: Negotiation Scripts

## Script 1: When You Get the Offer

**You:** "Thank you for the offer! I'm really excited about joining the team. I've reviewed the offer, and I was wondering if there's flexibility on the compensation. Based on my research and skills, I was hoping for something closer to Y."

**Recruiter:** "That's the best we can do for freshers."

**You:** "I understand. Besides the base salary, can we discuss other components like joining bonus, relocation, or additional benefits? I'm very keen on joining and want to find a package that works for both of us."

---

## Script 2: When You Have a Better Offer

**You:** "I'm very excited about your offer. However, I have another offer for X amount. Your company is my top choice because of [specific reason]. Is there room to match or get closer to that?"

**Why this works:** You're being honest, showing preference, and giving them a reason to increase the offer.

---

## Script 3: When the Offer is Below Market

**You:** "I appreciate the offer, and I really want to join your team. However, the current package is below the market range I've researched for this role and skill set. Based on my research, the typical range is X to Y. Is there flexibility to revisit the offer?"

---

## Script 4: Counter-Offer Template

**You:** "Thank you for the offer of [amount]. I'm very excited about this opportunity and strongly inclined to accept. However, based on my experience in [specific skills], the projects I've delivered, and my research on market rates, I was hoping for [target amount]. Could we meet somewhere closer to that?"

# Part 5: What to Ask For Beyond Salary

## Total Compensation Components

| Component | What It Is | How to Negotiate |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Base Salary | Monthly/yearly fixed pay | Primary negotiation target |
| Signing Bonus | One-time joining bonus | Ask if not offered |
| Performance Bonus | Yearly bonus based on performance | Clarify typical payout % |
| Stock Options / RSUs | Company equity | Important for startups |
| Relocation Support | Moving expenses | Ask if relocating |
| Education Budget | Courses, conferences | Ask if not mentioned |
| Flexible Hours | Work timing flexibility | Negotiate after offer |
| Remote Days | Work from home policy | Clarify company policy |
| Health Insurance | Medical coverage for family | Check what's covered |
| Gym / Wellness | Health perks | Ask about benefits |

## Script: How to Ask for Non-Salary Benefits

**You:** "The base salary sounds good. I was also wondering about the total benefits package - specifically, what does the company offer for learning and development, and is there a joining bonus?"

**Or:** "Could you share more details about the complete compensation structure including bonuses and stock options?"

# Part 6: How to Handle Low Offers

## Step 1: Don't Reject Immediately

**What to say:** "Thank you for the offer. Can I take a few days to review it carefully?"

## Step 2: Research and Evaluate

Check if the low offer is:
- Standard for your experience level (check Glassdoor)
- Low because of your negotiation position (no other offers)
- Low because of the company's budget constraints (startups)

## Step 3: Prepare Your Counter

**Scenario A: Offer is 20-30% below market**

**You:** "I've done my research, and the typical range for this role with my background is X-Y. I really want to join your team, but the current offer is below that. Can we explore a revised offer?"

**Scenario B: The company says "This is our fixed budget"**

**You:** "I understand budget constraints. In that case, could we discuss a signing bonus, or a performance review after 6 months with a guaranteed increase? I'm committed to proving my value."

## Step 4: Know When to Walk Away

If the offer is too low and they won't budge, it might be a sign that:
- The company doesn't value talent enough
- There won't be good growth opportunities
- Future raises will also be low

**Sometimes saying no is the best career move you can make.**

# Part 7: How to Ask for Time

## Asking for 3-5 Days to Decide

**You:** "Thank you for the offer! I'm very excited. Could you give me until [specific date] to review the details and get back to you?"

**If they push for a faster decision:**
"I understand you need a quick response. Would it be possible to have a quick call tomorrow to clarify a few things about the role and package, so I can make an informed decision?"

## When You're Waiting on Another Offer

**You:** "I'm very interested in your offer. I have another interview lined up that I'd like to complete before making a decision. Could I have until [date] to get back to you?"

**If they say no:**
"I understand. Let me review the offer tonight and get back to you tomorrow morning then."

# Part 8: Common Mistakes Freshers Make

## Mistake 1: Saying "I'll take anything"

**Why it's bad:** You set a low bar. They'll offer less than they were willing to pay.
**Better approach:** Show enthusiasm, but also show you know your worth.

---

## Mistake 2: Not Researching Beforehand

**Why it's bad:** You have no idea what's fair.
**Better approach:** Spend 1-2 hours researching on Glassdoor, LinkedIn, and talking to friends.

---

## Mistake 3: Negotiating Too Aggressively

**Why it's bad:** You might come across as entitled or greedy.
**Better approach:** Be polite, professional, and collaborative. Use phrases like "I'd love to find a number that works for both of us."

---

## Mistake 4: Focusing Only on Base Salary

**Why it's bad:** You miss out on stocks, bonuses, benefits that could be worth more.
**Better approach:** Consider the total compensation package.

---

## Mistake 5: Lying About Other Offers

**Why it's bad:** If they find out, you lose credibility and possibly the offer.
**Better approach:** Only mention actual offers. Never fabricate.

---

## Mistake 6: Accepting Too Quickly

**Why it's bad:** You leave money on the table. Most companies expect you to negotiate.
**Better approach:** Always ask for time to review, even if you're sure. Most freshers leave 10-20% on the table by not negotiating.

---

## Mistake 7: Negotiating via Email Without Tone

**Why it's bad:** Text without tone can sound demanding or rude.
**Better approach:** Request a phone call for negotiation. It's harder to say no on a call.

---

## Mistake 8: Comparing with Friends

**Why it's bad:** Everyone's situation is different. Your friend might have different skills or offers.
**Better approach:** Compare with market data, not individual anecdotes.

# Part 9: Real Dialogue Examples

## Example 1: Successful Negotiation

**Recruiter:** "We'd like to offer you the position. The package is 6 LPA."

**You:** "Thank you so much! I'm really excited about this opportunity. Based on my research, the market range for this role is between 7-10 LPA. Given my skills in Python, TensorFlow, and my project experience, is there flexibility in the offer?"

**Recruiter:** "Our budget for freshers is typically 6 LPA."

**You:** "I understand. Beyond base salary, would it be possible to discuss a joining bonus or a performance review at 6 months with a guaranteed increase? I'm very keen on joining."

**Recruiter:** "Let me check with the team. We might be able to offer a 50k joining bonus."

**You:** "That sounds good. I'd also appreciate if the offer letter could mention a 6-month performance review. I'm confident I'll prove my value by then."

**Result:** 6 LPA + 50k joining bonus + early performance review. Total first year: 6.5 LPA equivalent.

---

## Example 2: No Room to Negotiate

**Recruiter:** "The offer is 7 LPA. This is our final offer for this role."

**You:** "Thank you for being transparent. I really love the team and the work. Let me review the complete package and I'll get back to you in two days."

**Next day:** "I've reviewed the offer and I'm happy to accept. I'm excited to join and prove my value. Looking forward to the work we'll do together."

**Result:** Sometimes the offer is final, and that's okay. Take it if the role and growth opportunities are good.

---

## Example 3: Multiple Offers

**Company A:** "Our offer is 8 LPA."

**You:** "Thank you! I'm very excited. Currently, I have another offer for 10 LPA. Your company is my first choice because of the work you're doing in NLP. Would it be possible to get closer to 10?"

**Company A:** "We can do 9 LPA."

**You:** "That's very helpful. I'd appreciate if we could add a joining bonus as well. I'm ready to accept if we can work something out."

**Company A:** "We can offer a 75k joining bonus."

**You:** "That works for me. I'm happy to accept. Thank you!"

**Result:** 9 LPA + 75k joining bonus.

# Part 10: The Psychology of Negotiation

## Remember These Truths

1. **They want you.** They spent time and money interviewing you. They don't want to start over.

2. **Your silence is powerful.** After you make a request, wait for them to respond. Don't fill the silence.

3. **Be collaborative, not adversarial.** You're on the same team - finding a package that works.

4. **It's business, not personal.** Don't be afraid to ask. The worst they can say is no.

5. **Everything is negotiable.** Base salary, bonus, stocks, benefits, joining date - all can be discussed.

6. **Your first job sets a trajectory.** A 1 LPA difference now compounds over your career.
